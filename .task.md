# [MKT] Hero dual-CTA conflict: clarify primary path between ATS Scanner and Get Started

## Problem

The landing hero presents two equally weighted CTAs that compete for the same click:

- "Try Free ATS Scanner" (outline button) — links to `/ats-scanner`
- "Get Started" (gradient primary, with arrow) — links to `/sign-in`

The styling makes "Get Started" the primary, but the value proposition above the buttons ("Slothing remembers your full career history… writes a perfectly tailored resume…") aligns with neither tool specifically. Visitors hesitate between "try the free thing without signup" and "create an account first," and the page never resolves the conflict.

Additionally, "Get Started" is a generic verb that doesn't tell the visitor what happens after the click (sign-in screen).

## Repro

1. Open `http://localhost:3000/en`
2. Above the fold: two CTA buttons side-by-side, near-equal visual weight
3. Mental model: "Which one am I supposed to click?"

## Suspected cause / Root cause

`apps/web/src/app/[locale]/(marketing)/components/hero.tsx:52-72` — both buttons share `size="lg"` and sit in the same flex row with no hierarchy beyond color.

## Fix

Pick **one** primary action and demote the other. Two viable options:

**Option A — ATS Scanner is the hook (recommended given it's the only no-signup tool):**
- Primary: `Scan your resume free →` linking to `/ats-scanner`. Gradient/filled.
- Secondary: small "or `Create a free account`" text link below, linking to `/sign-in`.
- Removes the head-to-head button conflict; ATS scanner becomes the proven low-friction entry point.

**Option B — Account is the hook:**
- Primary: `Start free — no credit card →` to `/sign-in`. Gradient/filled.
- Secondary: outline `Try the ATS scanner →` to `/ats-scanner`, but smaller (`size="default"` not `size="lg"`).
- Subline below buttons: "Free forever. 5 tailored resumes / month."

Either way: tighten the primary CTA label from "Get Started" to something that promises a specific outcome ("Start free", "Tailor my first resume", "Scan my resume").
