# [PRICE] Pricing page: clarify Pro waitlist, add comparison/refund FAQ, fix awkward badge copy

## Problem

`/en/pricing` has three issues that compound to hurt conversion:

1. **Awkward badge copy** above the title: "Pro is opening from the waitlist" — grammatically off and unclear. A first-time visitor cannot tell if Pro is available now (mailto: button) or genuinely waitlisted.

2. **Pro CTA is a mailto link** (`mailto:waitlist@slothing.work?subject=Pro%20waitlist`) — modern visitors expect a form, not an email client popup. mailto: is also a known mobile UX failure (no email client / wrong one configured / corporate browsers strip it). Same problem on Student CTA (mailto:students@slothing.work).

3. **FAQ is too thin** (3 Qs) and misses the questions buyers actually have on this page:
   - Can I cancel anytime? (especially relevant given mailto-only purchase path)
   - Is the price USD? Are taxes included?
   - What happens to my Free tier resumes if I upgrade / cancel?
   - When does the Pro waitlist actually open? (no date promise anywhere)
   - Is there a refund policy?
   - Annual pricing?

4. **No visual comparison** between tiers — all three tier cards list different feature labels, so visitors can't quickly see what Pro adds over Free. The "Student" tier features overlap heavily with Pro but the user has to read three cards in sequence to confirm.

5. The `description` line for each tier has `min-h-14` for vertical alignment but the Free description ("Start tailoring better applications with no credit card.") and Pro ("For active searches where every application needs polish.") have very different lengths — Free wraps awkwardly on some viewports.

## Repro

1. Open `http://localhost:3000/en/pricing`
2. Read badge: "Pro is opening from the waitlist" → unclear status
3. Click "Join waitlist" on Pro → mailto popup, not a form
4. Scroll to FAQ → 3 thin questions, none about cancellation/billing/taxes

## Fix

1. **Badge copy:** Replace "Pro is opening from the waitlist" with one of:
   - "Pro is invite-only while billing ships — join the waitlist for early access."
   - "Pro launches soon — join the waitlist for first-day access."

2. **Replace mailto CTAs with a real waitlist form** (could be a simple POST to an internal endpoint that stores email + tier interest). If a form is out of scope for this ticket, at minimum: change copy from "Join waitlist" to "Email us for waitlist access" so the mailto behavior is expected, and add `target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer"` if it stays a mailto.

3. **Add FAQ entries**:
   - "Can I cancel Pro anytime?" — "Yes. Pro is monthly and you can cancel before the next renewal from your account settings."
   - "Are prices in USD?" — "Yes. Local taxes may apply depending on your billing country."
   - "What happens to my Free tier resumes if I upgrade?" — "All resumes you've already generated stay in your account. Upgrading just removes the monthly cap."
   - "When does Pro launch?" — only add if there's a real estimate; otherwise add "We'll email everyone on the waitlist when Pro is ready" to set expectations.

4. **Add a small comparison strip below the three tier cards** (or convert to a 3-column feature-matrix table) showing the 4-5 features that actually differ between tiers, so visitors don't have to read three cards to spot the delta.
