# [MKT] Remove fabricated testimonials (Sarah M., Jennifer L., Now at Google/HubSpot)

## Problem

`/en` testimonials section ships fake quotes attributed to invented people working at real companies:

- "Sarah M., Software Engineer, Now at Google"
- "Jennifer L., Marketing Director, Now at HubSpot"

Even with the small disclaimer "Stats, ratings, and testimonials are illustrative placeholders" below, attaching fake quotes to **named real companies** (Google, HubSpot) is a legal/PR risk and undermines the brand's credibility much more than it helps. A visitor who reads the quote then notices "illustrative" will trust nothing else on the page.

Equally problematic: the hero already has fake "10,000+ job seekers" + "4.9/5 rating" with the same illustrative disclaimer. Stacking three placeholder-but-presented-as-real signals is worse than zero social proof.

## Repro

1. Open `http://localhost:3000/en`
2. Scroll to "Trusted by job seekers everywhere"
3. See three stat cards with fabricated quotes + a separate testimonial card with another fake quote
4. Read tiny disclaimer below

## Suspected cause / Root cause

`apps/web/src/app/[locale]/(marketing)/components/testimonials.tsx:1-29, 70-86` — testimonial strings + author/company strings are hardcoded with invented names attributed to Google and HubSpot.

## Fix

Until real testimonials exist, replace this section with one of:

**Option 1 (recommended):** Replace with a "How Slothing helps" outcomes section — concrete capability claims, no fake people. Example cards:
- "ATS-aware tailoring" / "Slothing matches resume language against the exact ATS keywords most likely to be parsed."
- "One career profile, infinite resumes" / "Upload once. Generate a tailored resume for every role in under a minute."
- "Track every application" / "Capture, prioritize, and follow up — never lose a promising lead in your inbox."

**Option 2 (minimal change):** Delete `stats[].quote / .author / .role / .company` fields and the bottom testimonial card. Keep only the stat values but change them to truthful product facts (e.g. "5 free tailored resumes/month", "Free ATS scanner"). Drop the "illustrative" disclaimer since nothing remains illustrative.

DO NOT keep fake "Now at Google" / "Now at HubSpot" attributions, with or without disclaimer.

## Acceptance criteria

- [ ] No fabricated person names anywhere on `/en` (Sarah M., Jennifer L., etc. removed)
- [ ] No "Now at <real company name>" attribution on any quote/testimonial
- [ ] Either: real testimonials with signed permission, or product-feature cards (no people)
- [ ] `testimonials.test.tsx` updated
- [ ] `pnpm test` passes
