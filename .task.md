# [MKT] Marketing footer "Resources" links point at protected app routes (broken UX for logged-out visitors)

## Problem

The marketing footer "Resources" column links public visitors to authenticated-only app routes:

```
Dashboard      → /dashboard       (requires sign-in)
Documents      → /bank            (requires sign-in)
Interview Prep → /interview       (requires sign-in)
```

A logged-out visitor clicking "Documents" in the footer of `/en` gets bounced through the sign-in flow with a callback URL, then lands on an app screen that may be empty/onboarding. This is a confused-marketing-vs-app pattern that hurts conversion AND SEO (these links indicate to crawlers that those routes are public content).

Worse: there's no `/interview` route under `(marketing)`, and the `(app)/interview` route is not part of the public surface at all — it requires sign-in. The link is effectively a sign-in redirect dressed up as a content link.

## Repro

1. Open `http://localhost:3000/en` in a logged-out browser
2. Scroll to footer
3. Click any link under "Resources"
4. Either: redirected to sign-in, OR dropped into a half-onboarded app screen

## Suspected cause / Root cause

`apps/web/src/app/[locale]/(marketing)/components/footer.tsx:14-17` — the `resources` array points at `(app)` group routes that require auth, not at marketing/content pages.

## Fix

Replace the "Resources" column with content actually appropriate for the public surface. Two paths:

**Path A — drop the column:** Remove "Resources" entirely. Footer becomes 3-column (Brand + Product + Legal). Cleaner.

**Path B — populate with real public resources** as they exist:
- "ATS Scanner" → `/ats-scanner` (already public, currently under "Product" — could move here)
- "Browser Extension" → `/extension` (public)
- "Pricing" → `/pricing` (public)
- "Blog" / "Changelog" — only if those routes exist (extension page already references `/changelog` — verify that route exists before listing it)

Do NOT link to `/dashboard`, `/bank`, `/interview`, `/profile`, `/builder`, or any other `(app)`-group route from marketing footer.

While we're here: extension page also links to `/changelog` (`apps/web/src/app/[locale]/(marketing)/extension/page.tsx:253`) — verify that route exists; if not, remove the link or stub the page.

## Acceptance criteria

- [ ] No marketing footer link points at an authenticated-only `(app)`-group route
- [ ] All footer links resolve to public marketing or legal pages (no sign-in redirect)
